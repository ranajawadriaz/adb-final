
============================================
   DATABASE RECOVERY - EASY CHEAT SHEET
   (Undo/Redo with Checkpointing)
============================================

SETUP:  X=50, Y=100, Z=150 (starting values)
        5 transactions, system crashes at step 22
        Checkpoint happens at step 14


─────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 1 ➜ WHO NEEDS WHAT? (The Big Picture)
─────────────────────────────────────────

Think of it like a restaurant kitchen:
  - Some orders were FULLY cooked & served  ➜ REDO them (make sure they stick)
  - Some orders were HALF cooked            ➜ UNDO them (throw them out)
  - Some orders were done BEFORE closing    ➜ IGNORE them (already safe)


─────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 2 ➜ SORT THE TRANSACTIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────

  ✅ T2 → committed BEFORE checkpoint
         → IGNORE IT. Already safe. Done. Bye.

  ✅ T3, T5 → committed AFTER checkpoint
         → REDO these (re-apply their writes)

  ❌ T1, T4 → were still running when crash happened (never committed)
         → UNDO these (reverse their writes)


  HOW TO KNOW?
  - "commit" in the log = committed ✅
  - No "commit" before crash = not committed ❌
  - T2 committed at step 8 (before checkpoint at 14) → ignore
  - T3 committed at step 15 (after checkpoint) → redo
  - T5 committed at step 21 (after checkpoint) → redo
  - T1 never committed → undo
  - T4 never committed → undo


─────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 3 ➜ REDO (replay the good stuff)
─────────────────────────────────────────

  Only re-apply WRITE operations of T3 and T5
  Go TOP to BOTTOM through log:

    1. write_item(T3, Z, 125)     → Z becomes 125
    2. write_item(T5, Z, 110)     → Z becomes 110


─────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 4 ➜ UNDO (erase the bad stuff)
─────────────────────────────────────────

  Reverse WRITE operations of T1 and T4
  Go BOTTOM to TOP through log:

    1. write_item(T1, X, 25)      → X goes back to 25
                                     (wait, this IS the old value)

  T1 wrote X=25 at step 10, so undo puts X back.
  T4 had no writes that stuck.


─────────────────────────────────────────
STEP 5 ➜ FINAL VALUES AFTER RECOVERY
─────────────────────────────────────────

    ┌──────────┬──────────┐
    │  ITEM    │  VALUE   │
    ├──────────┼──────────┤
    │  X       │   25     │
    │  Y       │  125     │
    │  Z       │  110     │
    └──────────┴──────────┘


─────────────────────────────────────────
SUPER QUICK MEMORY TRICK 🧠
─────────────────────────────────────────

  1. Find the CHECKPOINT in the log
  2. Committed BEFORE checkpoint? → SKIP
  3. Committed AFTER checkpoint?  → REDO (replay writes, top→bottom)
  4. Never committed?             → UNDO (reverse writes, bottom→top)
  5. Apply changes → get final values

  REDO  = "do it again"   (for committed ones)
  UNDO  = "never happened" (for uncommitted ones)
